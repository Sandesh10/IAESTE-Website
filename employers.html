Employers

IAESTE SU welcomes association with corporates and potential empoyers. If you can offer us short internships or jobs and are willing to accept students from other countries. The possibilities are endless.

Mail Us at :iaeste.indiasu@gmail.com for more.

With 60 years of experience in arranging international work experience for young scientists, engineers and architects, the IAESTE programme can assist you in finding the right foreign student for internships and/or short or long term placements in your organisation.

The IAESTE programme is now represented in over 85 countries and has access to qualified students in all relevant science, engineering and technology sectors from in excess of 1000 universities.

An IAESTE Student Could

  • Create IT solutions
  • Undertake an energy or conservation study
  • Recommend design ideas
  • Explore overseas markets
  • Aid in research and development
  • Devise technical solutions
  • Advise on standards and qualifications in a target market
  • Design web pages
  • Develop new products or processes
  • Design structures
  • Conceptualise products
  • Execute production
  • Develop new business opportunities abroad
  • Provide useful international networking
  • Expose your staff to key overseas markets

WHY EMPLOY AN IAESTE STUDENT

  • IAESTE students are selected for their academic achievement
  • Employers specify trainee requirements, including skills or specialization, so that IAESTE can assign a ‘tailor-made’ trainee.
  • IAESTE students usually undergo previous training in research institutes or companies or on-site
  • Employers gain useful insights of overseas markets and work culture of other countries.
  • IAESTE students bring a conceptually strong theoretical knowledge to the table often enhanced by work in state-of-the-art laboratories and workshops.
  • Employers gain new perspectives to projects.
  • IAESTE students are self motivated and are truly passionate about work.

ROLE OF EMPLOYER

  • Specify all the requirements for acceptable candidates.
  • Review nomination submitted by IAESTE and indicate acceptance/rejection of candidate.
  • Arrange for proper supervision and training or work schedule during the training.
  • Offer a nominal salary to the student. You determine the salary, but it should at least cover living and lodging expenses.
  • Evaluation of the trainee at the end of the traineeship through an employer’s report.
  • Introduction to the assignments and to the people of the department in an effective way.
